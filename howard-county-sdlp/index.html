The basics

What is the Howard County SDLP?

The Settlement Downpayment Loan Program (SDLP) is run by the Howard County Department of Housing and Community Development. It provides down payment and closing cost assistance to eligible buyers purchasing a home anywhere in Howard County, from Columbia and Ellicott City to Elkridge, Laurel, and beyond.

Here is what makes the SDLP stand out among Maryland's county programs. First, the assistance is substantial: depending on the loan type you qualify for, help can reach up to $40,000. Second, the income limits are realistic for Howard County, ranging as high as $213,418 depending on household size and loan program. That means many buyers who assume they "make too much" for assistance actually qualify. Third, unlike many county programs that exhaust their budget mid-year, the SDLP rarely runs out of funds.

The program pairs with a fixed-rate primary mortgage from a participating lender. Your lender handles the application and reserves the funds directly with the county, which keeps the process smooth for you.

Five loans, one program

The 5 SDLP loan programs

The SDLP is really an umbrella that contains five different loan programs, and some of them include forgivable funds. Which one you qualify for depends on your household income and situation. Here is the quick map:

First-time buyers

HomeStarter Loan

For first-time homebuyers at the lower end of the SDLP income tiers, typically offering the deepest assistance.

HomeSteader Loan

For buyers purchasing in designated revitalization areas. You do not need to be a first-time buyer.

No first-time requirement

Workforce Initiative Loan

For eligible workforce buyers. You do not need to be a first-time buyer to use this option.

Do you qualify?

Basic SDLP eligibility

  • You are purchasing a home in Howard County as your primary residence
  • Your household income is within the limit for your household size and loan program (up to $213,418)
  • The purchase price is $683,977 or less
  • You have at least $1,000 to put toward the purchase, plus one month's mortgage payment (PITI) in savings
  • You lack sufficient funds to cover the full down payment and closing costs on your own
  • You are approved for a fixed-rate primary mortgage with a participating lender
  • First-time homebuyer status is required for HomeStarter, HomeSteader, and DreamMaker (but not for the Revitalization or Workforce Initiative loans)
  • You complete homebuyer education before settlement

Level up

Stack the SDLP with MIHU and statewide programs

Qualified buyers can combine the SDLP with Howard County's Moderate Income Housing Unit (MIHU) program, which allows select qualified applicants to purchase a home in Howard County at a reduced price. Layering a below-market purchase price with up to $40,000 in SDLP assistance is one of the most powerful affordability combinations anywhere in Maryland.

Quick answers

Howard County SDLP FAQ

How much down payment assistance can I get?
Depending on the SDLP loan type you qualify for, assistance can reach up to $40,000 toward your down payment and closing costs. The exact amount depends on your household income, the loan program, and your required down payment and closing costs.
Do I have to be a first-time homebuyer?
It depends on the loan. HomeStarter, HomeSteader, and DreamMaker require first-time homebuyer status. The Revitalization and Workforce Initiative loans do not.
What are the income limits?
Limits vary by household size and loan program, and currently range as high as $213,418, with a maximum purchase price of $683,977. Many Howard County buyers who assume they earn too much actually qualify, so it is worth checking your exact numbers.
Does the money have to be paid back?
SDLP assistance is structured as a loan with terms that vary by program, and some SDLP loan types include forgivable funds. In general there are no monthly payments, and the loan comes into play when you sell, move out, or refinance. I will walk you through the exact terms of the loan you qualify for.
Can I combine the SDLP with other programs?
Yes. Qualified buyers can stack the SDLP with Howard County's MIHU program, which offers select applicants a home at a reduced purchase price, and depending on your situation, statewide programs may fit too.
How fast can I close?
The SDLP is more streamlined than many county programs. With an experienced lender, closings in as little as 30 days are possible, which can be the difference-maker in a multiple-offer situation.
